Asia Kakehashi Project Scholar, 2021

Age 16 — One of 21 from India, 250 across Asia

Selection & Program

Selected by the Japanese government for a fully-funded, year-long cultural ambassadorship program with stipend. Competed against thousands of Indian applicants for one of 21 spots representing the country.

Academic Placement

Completed full academic year at Yokohama Hiranuma High School. Not an exchange observer — fully embedded in Japanese education system, attending classes in Japanese, competing academically in second-language environment.

Academic Achievement: Scored 2nd highest in school-wide English examination among all students.

Cultural Immersion

  • Kyuudo (Japanese Archery): Traditional martial art rooted in Zen philosophy
  • Tea Ceremony: Formal ceremonial practice requiring technical mastery
  • Calligraphy & Stamp Carving: Traditional art forms practiced at intermediate level
  • Athletics: Basketball, badminton, annual marathon training as regular student activities

Impact & Takeaway

This wasn't cultural tourism — it was operational-depth immersion. Living with a host family, navigating a foreign education system, practicing martial arts, and performing academically in Japanese at age 16 developed cross-cultural fluency that cannot be taught theoretically.

The experience fundamentally shaped my understanding of international relations: genuine diplomatic capability emerges from lived experience in foreign systems, not academic study of them.
