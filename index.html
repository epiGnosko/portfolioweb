Asia Kakehashi Project Scholar, 2021

Age 16 — One of 21 from India, 250 across Asia

Selected by the Japanese government for a fully-funded, year-long cultural ambassadorship program with stipend. Completed full academic year at Yokohama Hiranuma High School, embedded in the Japanese education system.

Trained in kyuudo (Japanese archery), traditional calligraphy, stamp carving, and tea ceremony while competing in basketball, badminton, and annual marathon. Scored 2nd highest in school-wide English examination.

This wasn't cultural tourism—it was cultural immersion at operational depth. Living with a host family, attending classes in Japanese, practicing martial arts rooted in Zen philosophy, and navigating a foreign education system at 16 developed cross-cultural fluency that can't be taught in classrooms.

The experience fundamentally shaped my understanding of international relations: that genuine diplomatic capability emerges from lived experience in foreign systems, not theoretical study of them.
